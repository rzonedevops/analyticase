This theory has gained significant traction in problem-solving courts (drug courts, mental health courts, domestic violence courts) and represents a paradigm shift toward holistic justice.

2. **Postmodern Legal Theory** - Law is indeterminate text without fixed meaning, subject to deconstruction. This theory challenges traditional legal reasoning and grand narratives, emphasizing the role of language, power, and interpretation in legal discourse.

3. **Comparative Law Theory** - Study of different legal systems reveals diversity of legal solutions and common principles. This theory enables legal transplants, harmonization, and better understanding of law in a globalized world, with particular relevance to EU law, international commercial law, and constitutional borrowing.

### Level 1 (First-Order Principles) - Known Laws

**File**: `lex/lv1/known_laws_v2.3.scm`

**Total Principles**: 70 (increased from 60 in v2.1)

**New Principles Added**:

#### Statutory Interpretation Principles (3 new)

1. **Lex Specialis Derogat Legi Generali** - Special law derogates from general law; specific provisions prevail over general ones. This fundamental interpretive canon is essential for resolving conflicts between general and specific legislation, with applications in constitutional law, administrative law, and international law.

2. **Expressio Unius Est Exclusio Alterius** - Expression of one thing excludes another; mention of specific items excludes others. This canon is applied with caution in modern purposive interpretation but remains important for interpreting lists and enumerations in statutes and contracts.

3. **Ejusdem Generis** - Of the same kind; general words following specific words are limited to the same class. This traditional rule of construction is now integrated with purposive interpretation approaches.

#### Equity and Fairness Principles (3 new)

4. **In Pari Delicto** - In equal fault; parties equally at fault cannot claim against each other. This equity principle has evolved from strict application to a more nuanced approach balanced with public policy and restitution.

5. **Ubi Jus Ibi Remedium** - Where there is a right, there is a remedy; every legal right has a corresponding remedy. This foundational maxim underpins access to justice and has expanded to include innovative and structural remedies in constitutional law.

6. **Ignorantia Juris Non Excusat** - Ignorance of the law is no excuse; lack of knowledge of law does not excuse violation. This universal principle is now balanced with fair notice requirements and accessibility of law, particularly for complex regulatory offenses.

#### Agency and Vicarious Liability Principles (1 new)

7. **Qui Facit Per Alium Facit Per Se** - He who acts through another acts himself; basis of agency and vicarious liability. This principle has expanded from traditional agency law to corporate criminal liability and modern vicarious liability doctrines.

#### De Minimis and Fraud Principles (2 new)

8. **De Minimis Non Curat Lex** - The law does not concern itself with trifles; trivial matters are not actionable. This practical limitation on litigation is applied to standing and justiciability, balanced with access to justice concerns.

9. **Fraus Omnia Corrumpit** - Fraud corrupts everything; fraud vitiates all transactions and proceedings. This absolute principle has been extended to abuse of process and litigation misconduct, with highest conflict priority.

#### Enhanced Existing Principle

10. **Volenti Non Fit Injuria** - Enhanced with improved metadata, additional case law, and cross-references to new principles (in-pari-delicto). The principle's evolution from absolute defense to qualified application is now fully documented.

**Enhancements to Existing Principles**:

- **Derivation Chains**: All principles now explicitly reference their Level 2 meta-principle derivations.
- **Case Law Updates**: Added 2024-2025 case law for 30+ existing principles, including recent South African cases.
