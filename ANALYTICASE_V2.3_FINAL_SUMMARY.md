### Level 2 Meta-Principles (Jurisprudential Theories)

#### New Theories Added (3)

##### 1. Therapeutic Jurisprudence
- **Description**: Law should be studied for its therapeutic and anti-therapeutic consequences, promoting psychological well-being through legal rules and procedures
- **Historical Origin**: Developed in 1990s by David Wexler and Bruce Winick
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Applied in problem-solving courts (drug courts, mental health courts, domestic violence courts)
- **Influence Score**: 0.85
- **Key Applications**: Sexual offences courts, victim-centered approaches, rehabilitation programs
- **Cross-References**: Restorative justice theory, ethic of care, ubuntu philosophy

##### 2. Postmodern Legal Theory
- **Description**: Law is indeterminate text without fixed meaning, subject to deconstruction
- **Historical Origin**: Emerged in late 20th century from poststructuralist philosophy
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Challenges traditional legal reasoning and grand narratives
- **Influence Score**: 0.72
- **Key Applications**: Critical analysis of legal discourse, power dynamics in law
- **Cross-References**: Critical legal studies, feminist legal theory

##### 3. Comparative Law Theory
- **Description**: Study of different legal systems reveals diversity of legal solutions and common principles
- **Historical Origin**: Emerged in 19th century with comparative legal studies
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Essential for legal transplants, harmonization, globalization
- **Influence Score**: 0.88
- **Key Applications**: S v Makwanyane (1995) - comparative constitutional analysis, EU law harmonization
- **Cross-References**: Legal positivism, natural law theory

   - Conflict Priority: 75

3. **Ejusdem Generis**
   - Latin: "Ejusdem generis"

   - Domain: Statutory interpretation
   - Conflict Priority: 70
